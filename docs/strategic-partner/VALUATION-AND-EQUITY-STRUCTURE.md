## Abstract

This document outlines a proposed pre-money valuation range of **CAD $1.5 million to $2.2 million** (target approximately **$1.8 million**) and a balanced equity and governance structure for the seed financing of Work Chop Inc.'s MediForge Electronic Medical Records (EMR) platform.

The analysis is grounded in 2025–2026 industry benchmarks for early-stage healthtech SaaS, MVP development costs, standard founder retention data, and common market practices.

The structure recognizes the Founder's completed development work while providing the **Strategic Partner** meaningful ownership, economic upside, and appropriate protections. It is designed to align incentives for long-term success and **mutual benefit** between the Founder and Strategic Partner.

## Introduction

Work Chop Inc. has developed MediForge, a proprietary EMR platform for physicians, clinics, and hospitals. The product is **deployed and in active use** as a multi-clinic platform (not a prototype). Through **June 2026**, Phases **0–8** of Ontario EMR readiness software are complete where possible: documented readiness is **72–82%** (see [Ontario Readiness Report](https://mediforge.netlify.app/ontario-readiness)). Live provincial pipes and OntarioMD Stage 5 validation remain partner- and credential-gated.

This document provides a transparent, data-driven overview of the proposed valuation, equity allocation, development fee, and governance terms to support informed discussions between the **Founder**, the **Strategic Partner**, and clinical partners.

## Valuation Justification

A pre-money valuation range of **CAD $1.5 million to $2.2 million** (target ~$1.8 million) is proposed. This reflects the current stage of the Company and balances risk and opportunity for all parties.

The proposed pre-money valuation is realistic for a pre-revenue healthtech SaaS platform with a **working product and substantial provincial integration software**. It is derived from three complementary approaches.

### 1. Cost / Replacement Approach

Rebuilding a compliant EMR with similar features, security, and integrations would cost significantly more today.

Developing a compliant healthtech EMR/EHR platform typically costs **$80,000–$350,000+** in 2025–2026, with many estimates in the **$150,000–$300,000** range for features including security, integrations, and regulatory readiness. MediForge now exceeds MVP scope: MCEDT claims desk, OLIS-ready lab desk, PrescribeIT-ready eRx queue, DIR-ready imaging desk, HRM inbox with chart filing, DHDR hooks, interop gateway, and compliance pack. The Founder has already invested substantial capital; technology risk for the Strategic Partner has been **substantially reduced**.

#### Estimated Platform Replacement Cost Breakdown

| Category | Cost Range (CAD) | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| Core Development | $60k – $120k | UI/UX, back end, database |
| Compliance and Security | $30k – $80k | PHIPA, encryption, audits |
| Key Integrations (labs, claims, eRx, HRM, DHDR) | $40k – $80k | Provincial desks built; live credentials separate |
| Testing and Certification Prep | $10k – $40k | Evidence binder, self-assessment, 75+ interop tests |
| **Total Replacement Cost** | **$140k – $320k+** | Industry benchmarks (2025–2026); MediForge exceeds MVP |

The completed platform substantially de-risks the partnership and supports a valuation well above raw build cost.

### 2. Market Comparables and Benchmarks

Pre-seed and early-seed SaaS valuations commonly range from **$500,000 to $5 million**. Healthtech companies often command a premium due to regulatory requirements and large addressable markets.

| Stage / Segment | Typical Pre-Money Valuation | Source |
|-----------------|----------------------------|--------|
| Pre-seed SaaS MVP | $0.5M – $5M | OpenVC |
| Early Healthtech (MVP stage) | $1M – $4M+ | FlowJam healthtech premium |
| **MediForge Proposed Range** | **$1.5M – $2.2M** | Conservative fit for deployed platform + Ontario software |

### 3. Berkus / Scorecard Method

Strong scores on **working product**, **market opportunity** (large global EMR/EHR sector), **Canadian regulatory readiness** (72–82% documented Ontario readiness), and **execution** support the proposed valuation.

| Element | Value Contribution | Rationale |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| Market Opportunity | $500k – $800k | Large growing EMR sector |
| Working Product (beyond MVP) | $600k – $1.2M | Deployed EMR + Phases 0–8 provincial software |
| Execution and Compliance | $400k – $700k | PHIPA pack, gap report, evidence binder, interop tests |
| **Total** | **$1.5M – $2.7M** | Supports proposed range |

The target of **~$1.8 million** represents a balanced, defensible valuation that rewards progress made while leaving room for strong Strategic Partner returns as the Company scales.

## Equity Split and Governance

The proposed post-closing structure is designed to be fair, balanced, and aligned with industry norms:

| Category | Percentage | Description |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Founder (Yinka Daramola) | 65–75% | Common shares with voting rights; development contributions and ongoing leadership |
| Strategic Partner | 20–30% | Preferred shares providing economic upside and standard protections |
| Option Pool | 10–15% | Reserved for future employees and advisors |

### Alignment with Market Data

- Median founder team ownership after a typical seed round is approximately **56%** (Carta data via CRV, 2026).
- Typical seed dilution is **15–25%**. The proposed split is achievable with a modest raise and supports long-term alignment.

### Governance and Control

- **Board composition:** Balanced representation with Founder leadership as CEO. The Strategic Partner receives appropriate board or observer rights.
- **Voting and decision rights:** Founder retains operational control while the Strategic Partner has standard protective provisions for major corporate actions (e.g., sale of the Company or significant new debt).
- **Vesting:** Standard 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff applies to all shares to protect the Company and align incentives.

These terms follow common practices that balance founder execution with Strategic Partner protections (Founders Network, 2026).

## Upfront Development Fee

A one-time development fee of **CAD $100,000–$120,000** is proposed. This compensates for the Founder's pre-financing development work that created the deployed platform and de-risked the opportunity for all parties. Such fees are a standard and transparent way to recognize pre-seed contributions in founder-led companies.

The fee does not need to be paid fully upfront. However, a **minimum of 40%** shall be paid at closing. The balance (60% or less) may be structured using one or more of the following transparent, Strategic-Partner-friendly options, as mutually agreed in the definitive agreements:

**Option 1: Interest-Bearing Promissory Note (Deferred Cash Payment)**  
The unpaid balance documented as a short-term loan from the Founder to the Company. Simple interest at a market rate (e.g., 8–12% per annum), repaid in equal monthly or quarterly installments over 12–18 months.

**Option 2: Convertible Note (Conversion into Equity)**  
The unpaid balance may convert into equity at the next qualified financing round at a discount (e.g., 15–25%) or using a pre-agreed valuation cap.

**Option 3: Hybrid Structure**  
A portion as an interest-bearing note, with the remainder satisfied through a small additional equity grant to the Founder or a capped revenue share (e.g., 1–3% of gross revenue until paid, with a maximum cap).

All structures include a hard deadline: full liquidation within **18 months** or upon a liquidity event, whichever occurs first.
