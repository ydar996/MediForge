1. Services Provided

Work Chop grants you a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to access and use MediForge, a cloud-hosted and offline-capable Progressive Web App for electronic health records, appointment scheduling, clinical documentation, prescription management, billing, and analytics.

2. Compliance with Laws and Standards

2.1 Regulatory framework — Canada first

MediForge is built primarily for clinics in Canada. Work Chop operates MediForge in compliance with Canadian health privacy law as the default framework. Where you also serve US patients or are subject to US law, the supplemental Business Associate Agreement (BAA) below applies in addition—not in place of—the Canadian Privacy and Health Information Addendum.

Depending on where you practice and where your patients reside, applicable law includes:

  • Canada (federal) — primary: Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), where it applies to commercial activities involving personal information.
  • Canada (provincial) — primary: Health information privacy statutes such as PHIPA (Ontario), HIA (Alberta), HIA (British Columbia health sector), and equivalent provincial laws. Your clinic is the health information custodian (or equivalent); Work Chop is your agent or service provider.
  • United States — supplemental: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), including the Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules, and applicable state health privacy laws—only where you are a US covered entity or process US PHI.
  • Security and operations: Safeguards aligned with Canadian health-privacy expectations, ISO 27001 controls, encryption of data at rest and in transit, role-based access, audit logging, and breach response procedures appropriate for healthcare software.
  • Interoperability: Clinical data exchange standards (including FHIR where enabled) aligned with Canada Health Infoway pan-Canadian standards; US ONC-aligned workflows where applicable for US operations.

No separate government “EMR vendor licence” is generally required for Work Chop to provide MediForge in Canada. Compliance is achieved through contractual safeguards, security controls, and your obligations as custodian—not through a single vendor permit.

2.2 Roles and incorporated agreements

  • All subscribers: The Canadian Privacy and Health Information Addendum below is MediForge’s primary health-information privacy agreement and is incorporated by reference for every clinic using the platform.
  • US subscribers or US PHI: Where HIPAA applies, Work Chop also acts as a Business Associate. The supplemental BAA below applies in addition to the Canadian Addendum for that US-regulated information.
  • Mixed or cross-border practices: Canadian law governs Canadian patient records; US law governs US patient records. Where both apply, the stricter applicable standard controls for that record.

2.3 Cross-border data storage and transfers

MediForge may store and process data using cloud infrastructure. Work Chop’s baseline commitment is protection comparable to PIPEDA and provincial health privacy law. Where information is stored or processed outside Canada, Work Chop remains accountable to you as custodian and will implement contractual and technical safeguards accordingly.

  • PIPEDA generally permits cross-border processing when the custodian remains accountable and comparable protection is maintained.
  • Some provinces impose additional expectations for health information (for example, stricter rules in British Columbia and Nova Scotia regarding storage location). You are responsible for determining whether your province requires Canadian data residency or additional patient notice, and for configuring your subscription accordingly where options are offered.
  • Work Chop will notify subscribers of material changes to primary hosting regions where reasonably practicable.

2.4 Provincial programs and certification

Certain provinces maintain programs for EMR certification or integration with provincial systems (for example, OntarioMD in Ontario). MediForge may support conformance testing or integrations where available, but provincial certification or connection to a provincial EHR is not automatic upon subscription. You are responsible for meeting any program requirements applicable to your practice location.

3. Your Obligations as Health Information Custodian

3.1

You remain the owner and controller (or health information custodian) of all patient health information and personal information entered into MediForge.

3.2

You are solely responsible for:

  • Obtaining valid patient consent or other lawful authority before collecting or entering health information, as required in your province or state
  • Maintaining accurate clinical records and appropriate clinical decision-making
  • Using MediForge only for lawful medical practice and permitted healthcare operations
  • Notifying Work Chop without undue delay of any suspected security incident or privacy breach involving the platform
  • Notifying affected individuals and regulators as required by PIPEDA, PHIPA, provincial law, or HIPAA where applicable (custodian/covered entity responsibility)
  • Completing privacy impact assessments or other assessments where required by your jurisdiction

3.3

You must appoint at least one administrator and maintain up-to-date staff roles and access rights.

4. Security and Confidentiality

4.1

We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards designed to meet Canadian health-privacy expectations and, where applicable, the HIPAA Security Rule.

4.2

All data at rest is encrypted (AES-256). Data in transit uses TLS 1.3.

4.3

You agree to use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication where offered, and not share login credentials.

5. Subscription, Fees and Payment

5.1

Subscription plans and fees are published at Manage Subscription. Canadian fees may be shown in CAD; US fees in USD, as indicated on the pricing page.

5.2

Fees are payable monthly or annually in advance. Late payments may result in suspension after 14 days' notice.

6. Uptime and Support

We target 99.5% monthly uptime excluding scheduled maintenance. Support is provided via email and in-app chat during standard business hours.

7. Termination and Data Retrieval

7.1

Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice.

7.2

Upon termination, you may export all your data in standard formats (CSV, PDF, FHIR where available) within 60 days at no extra charge. After 60 days, data may be permanently deleted.

8. Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, Work Chop's total liability shall not exceed the fees paid by you in the 12 months preceding any claim. We are not liable for clinical decisions or outcomes.

9. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

For clinics practicing in Canada, this Agreement and the Canadian Privacy and Health Information Addendum are interpreted under the laws of your province or territory of practice, together with applicable federal Canadian law. Where those laws cannot be contracted away, they prevail over any conflicting term.

For clinics practicing solely in the United States, or where US law expressly applies to specific records, governing law includes applicable US federal and state law and the supplemental BAA.

Work Chop Inc. is a California corporation; procedural matters (service of process, arbitration venue) may be handled under California law except where prohibited by Canadian consumer or privacy law. Disputes shall first be submitted to mediation. If unresolved, binding arbitration under AAA rules applies except where prohibited by applicable law.

10. Entire Agreement

This document, together with the Canadian Privacy and Health Information Addendum (primary), the supplemental Business Associate Agreement (US) where applicable, and the current Pricing page, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

Disclaimer: This agreement summarizes common compliance topics with a Canada-first framework. It is not legal advice. Consult qualified counsel in your province or state regarding your specific obligations.

By proceeding, you confirm that you have the authority to bind your medical practice and that you accept these terms on behalf of yourself and all authorized users in your organization.

CANADIAN PRIVACY AND HEALTH INFORMATION ADDENDUM

Primary agreement — PIPEDA and Provincial Health Information Privacy Laws

This is MediForge’s primary health-information privacy framework. All clinics accept this Addendum. US-only HIPAA terms appear separately in the supplemental BAA below, where applicable.

This Addendum is incorporated into the MediForge Service Subscription and Data Processing Agreement between Work Chop Inc. and your clinic.

1. Roles

You are the health information custodian (or organization accountable under applicable provincial law) for personal health information you enter, collect, use, or disclose through MediForge. Work Chop is your agent or information manager / service provider, processing information only on your documented instructions and as permitted by this Addendum and the Main Agreement.

2. Permitted processing

Work Chop may access, use, store, and disclose personal health information only to:

  • Provide MediForge services described in the Main Agreement
  • Maintain security, backups, and disaster recovery
  • Comply with lawful requests and applicable Canadian law
  • De-identify or aggregate data where agreed in writing, if ever permitted by you

3. Safeguards

Work Chop will implement safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of health information, including access controls, encryption, audit trails, staff confidentiality obligations, and incident response procedures designed to meet or exceed common PIPEDA and provincial health-privacy expectations.

4. Cross-border processing

Where information is processed or stored outside Canada, Work Chop remains accountable under this Addendum and will contractually require subprocessors to provide comparable protection. You acknowledge that some provinces impose additional requirements (including limitations on storage location in British Columbia and Nova Scotia). You are responsible for determining whether cross-border processing is permitted for your practice and for providing any required notice to patients.

5. Breach and security incidents

Work Chop will notify you without undue delay after becoming aware of a security incident involving unauthorized access to personal health information in MediForge, and will cooperate with your investigation. You remain responsible for notifying the Privacy Commissioner of Canada, provincial commissioners, or other regulators and affected individuals as required by PIPEDA, PHIPA, or other applicable law.

6. Access, correction, and retention

Work Chop will assist you in responding to individual access and correction requests using MediForge export and record-management tools. Upon termination, you may export records in standard formats; Work Chop will delete or return information in accordance with the Main Agreement and your written instructions, subject to legal retention requirements.

7. Subprocessors

Work Chop may use subprocessors (cloud hosting, email, support tools) that process personal health information on our behalf. We require subprocessors to implement appropriate safeguards and limit use to authorized purposes.

8. Audit and compliance

Upon reasonable notice, Work Chop will provide information about security practices relevant to your compliance obligations. Formal audits may be conducted no more than once per year unless required by law or following a material incident.

9. Provincial variations

Requirements under PHIPA (Ontario), Alberta HIA, BC health-sector rules, and other provincial statutes may impose additional duties on custodians. This Addendum does not replace those duties. Where a provincial law imposes a stricter standard, the stricter standard applies to the extent required by law.

10. Term

This Addendum is accepted electronically together with the Main Agreement and continues until terminated. Obligations regarding confidentiality and security survive termination.

By accepting the Main Agreement, you accept this Addendum as MediForge’s primary health-information privacy framework on behalf of your custodianship of personal health information processed through the platform.

BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT (BAA)

Supplemental — United States HIPAA only

Applies only when you are a US covered entity or business associate subject to HIPAA, or when you process Protected Health Information governed by US law. It supplements—not replaces—the Canadian Privacy and Health Information Addendum above.

This Business Associate Agreement ("BAA") is entered into as of the date You accept the MediForge Service Subscription and Data Processing Agreement ("Main Agreement") between:

Work Chop Inc.
(a California corporation with principal office in San Diego, CA, USA)
("Business Associate" or "Work Chop")

You / Your Clinic / Your Practice

WHEREAS

Covered Entity is a healthcare provider subject to HIPAA and wishes to use MediForge, a cloud-based electronic health record and practice management system operated by Work Chop.

Work Chop will receive, create, maintain, transmit, or otherwise have access to Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of Covered Entity.

The Parties enter into this BAA to comply with 45 C.F.R. §§ 164.502(e) and 164.504(e) and to satisfy the Business Associate contract requirements of HIPAA.

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Definitions

Terms used but not defined herein shall have the meaning given in the HIPAA Rules (45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164).

2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures of PHI

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only as necessary to perform the services set forth in the Main Agreement or as Required by Law.

Business Associate may use PHI for its proper management and administration and to fulfill legal responsibilities.

3. Obligations of Business Associate

Business Associate agrees to:

  • a. Not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this BAA or as Required by Law.
  • b. Implement and maintain administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that meet or exceed the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 164, Subpart C).
  • c. Report to Covered Entity, without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 10 business days, any Security Incident or Breach of Unsecured PHI of which it becomes aware.
  • d. Enter into Business Associate Agreements with subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on its behalf.
  • e. Make available PHI for amendment and incorporate amendments as directed by Covered Entity.
  • f. Make its internal practices, books, and records relating to PHI available to the Secretary of HHS for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with HIPAA.
  • g. Upon termination, return or destroy all PHI (if feasible). If not feasible, extend protections of this BAA to retained PHI.

4. Obligations of Covered Entity

Covered Entity shall:

  • a. Notify Business Associate of any limitation(s) in its Notice of Privacy Practices that affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.
  • b. Obtain any patient authorizations or consents required under applicable law before sharing PHI with Business Associate (where not already covered by treatment, payment, or healthcare operations).
  • c. Not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate HIPAA.

5. Term and Termination

5.1

This BAA commences on the date Covered Entity accepts the Main Agreement and continues until terminated.

5.2

Covered Entity may terminate this BAA and the Main Agreement immediately if Business Associate has breached a material term and fails to cure within 30 days of notice.

5.3

Upon termination, Business Associate shall return or destroy all PHI. If return or destruction is infeasible, protections continue until PHI is returned or destroyed.

6. Data Ownership and Export

All PHI remains the property of Covered Entity. Upon request or termination, Business Associate will provide PHI in a mutually agreed standard format (CSV, PDF, or FHIR where available) at no additional charge.

7. Indemnification

Each Party shall indemnify the other against losses arising from its breach of this BAA or willful violation of HIPAA.

8. Miscellaneous

8.1

This BAA is governed by the laws of the State of California and federal HIPAA law.

8.2

Amendment: This BAA will be deemed amended to comply with changes in HIPAA or other applicable law.

8.3

Survival: Obligations regarding PHI survive termination.
